HYPERTENSION (HIGH BLOOD PRESSURE) DIETARY GUIDELINES — Based on DASH Diet and ACC/AHA Guidelines

SODIUM:
Reducing sodium is the single most important dietary change for blood pressure control. Target less than 1500-2300mg of sodium per day. One teaspoon of salt contains approximately 2300mg sodium. High sodium foods include processed meats, canned soups, salty snacks, pickles, and fast food. Even small reductions in sodium intake can meaningfully lower blood pressure.

POTASSIUM:
Potassium helps counteract the effects of sodium on blood pressure. Good sources include bananas, sweet potatoes, spinach, beans, and avocados. Target 3500-5000mg potassium per day.

THE DASH DIET:
The Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension (DASH) diet is clinically proven to lower blood pressure. It emphasizes fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean protein, and low-fat dairy. It limits red meat, sweets, and sugary beverages. The DASH diet can lower systolic blood pressure by 8-14 mmHg.

SATURATED FAT:
High saturated fat intake contributes to arterial stiffness and elevated blood pressure. Limit saturated fat to less than 6% of total calories. Avoid full-fat dairy, fatty cuts of red meat, butter, and coconut oil.

ALCOHOL:
Excess alcohol raises blood pressure. Men should limit to 2 drinks per day, women to 1 drink per day.

MAGNESIUM AND CALCIUM:
Both minerals play a role in blood pressure regulation. Sources of magnesium include nuts, seeds, and dark leafy greens. Calcium sources include low-fat dairy, fortified plant milk, and leafy greens.
