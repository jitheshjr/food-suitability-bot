CHRONIC KIDNEY DISEASE (CKD) DIETARY GUIDELINES — Based on National Kidney Foundation Recommendations

PROTEIN:
In CKD, the kidneys cannot adequately filter waste products from protein metabolism. Reducing protein intake slows kidney disease progression. For non-dialysis CKD patients, limit protein to 0.6-0.8g per kg body weight per day. High protein foods to limit include red meat, poultry, fish, dairy, and eggs. Plant proteins from legumes may be better tolerated.

SODIUM:
Damaged kidneys struggle to excrete excess sodium, leading to fluid retention, swelling, and high blood pressure. Limit sodium to less than 2000mg per day in CKD. Avoid processed foods, canned items, and restaurant meals which are typically very high in sodium.

POTASSIUM:
When kidneys fail, potassium builds up in the blood causing dangerous heart rhythm problems. High potassium foods to avoid include bananas, oranges, potatoes, tomatoes, and avocados. Low potassium alternatives include apples, berries, grapes, and white rice.

PHOSPHORUS:
Damaged kidneys cannot remove excess phosphorus, which pulls calcium from bones and weakens them. Limit phosphorus-rich foods including dairy products, nuts, seeds, whole grains, and cola drinks. Phosphorus additives in processed foods are especially problematic as they are almost entirely absorbed.

FLUIDS:
In advanced CKD, fluid restriction may be necessary to prevent fluid overload. This is highly individual and should be guided by a nephrologist.

CALORIES:
Maintaining adequate calorie intake is important to prevent malnutrition in CKD. Refined carbohydrates like white rice and pasta are acceptable calorie sources as they are low in potassium and phosphorus.
